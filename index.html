George "Buzz" Beurling: The Falcon Of Malta

The Canadian Ace and Malta Defender.

The Early Life of George Beurling

George Beurling was born in Verdun, Quebec, in 1921. From a young age, he showed a passion for aviation and was known for his exceptional vision and sharpshooting skills.

Joining the Royal Air Force

In 1942, Beurling joined the Royal Air Force and was deployed to defend Malta, a crucial Allied base in the Mediterranean.

The Heroism in Malta

Known for his fearless tactics and unmatched accuracy, Beurling downed 27 enemy aircraft in Malta, solidifying his legendary status.

Earning the Name "Screwball"

Beurling's unconventional style earned him the nickname "Screwball." He was awarded multiple honors, including the Distinguished Flying Medal and the Distinguished Service Order.

Post-War Life and Legacy

After the war, Beurling struggled to adapt to civilian life. He later joined the Israeli Air Force but tragically died in a plane crash in 1948.

Impact and Remembrance

Today, Beurling is celebrated as one of Canada’s greatest fighter aces, inspiring respect and admiration worldwide.
